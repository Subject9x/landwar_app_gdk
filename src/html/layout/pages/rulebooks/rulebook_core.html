Introduction!

Welcome to LANDWAR, the miniatures-agnostic, theme-agnostic, skirmish wargame rules set! These rules are designed to allow a wide range of miniatures to interact in 1 set of rules. From new wargame players starting out, to veterans with dozens of miniatures, LANDWAR provides an approachable and flexible combat system for 1-2hr skirmishes. The rules are also considered open-source, we encourage players to modify and tweak rules as they play, fun being the most important element of the game.

This rulebook covers the absolute basics needed to start and play a session of LANDWAR. Unit stats, the info cards, the phases of a turn, and how to make attacks. Scenarios, Special Abilities, and starting army lists are provided elsewhere for sake of brevity.

Glossary

"May"Player can choose whether or not to take an action.
"Must"Player has no choice and action/event is executed.
ForceThe group of Units a Player controls during gameplay.
Modelrefers to the physical models on the table during gameplay.
UnitMeans a unit as represented by a Unit Info Card. A Unit might consist of 1 or more Models.
INITotal sum of dice rolls and modifiers for a Player made in the Initiative Phase.
ATKThe number of dice a player rolls for an attack in the Combat Phase.
DEFThe number of dice a player rolls for defense against an attack in the Combat Phase.
Line of Sightabbreviated LoS, a line drawn by a player between 2 points on the game board, inside the play area.
AdvantagePlayer who has the higher Initiative value, they will choose to move first or last, and always attacks first.
Effective RangeRange in LANDWAR is unlimited, but each Unit has a range segment where they do not suffer penalties for a long range attack.
FacingUsed in various checks and actions, defines an angle or arc of angle for models.
Forward ArcUsed in various checks and actions, this is the front 180 degress of the model.
Rear ArcUsed in various checks and actions, this is the rear 180 degress of the model.
TargetA Unit chosen by an attacking Player.

Unit Info Card

All Units in a game of LANDWAR are defined by their Unit Card. The unit card contains all relevant stats and special abilities for each unit in the game. Unit cards may represent 1 or more models that are on the table during play.

Core Stats
NameUnique identifier for the unit.
SizeAbstract representation of a unit's overall size, used for some action and ability checks.
MoveA Unit's normal move range, listed in inches.
EvadeUnit's base DEF modifier if the Unit moved during the Movement Phase. Can be 0.
ArmorUnit's health pool, reduced as Unit takes damage from sources. When 0, Unit is destroyed.
PointsThe cost of the unit in points. If players agree to use a points cost, then this value is used.
MeleeThe starting amount of damage a Unit deals in Melee Range(0 - 1"). Can be 0.
Ranged AttackPrinted as (damage) - (range in inches), starting amount of damage at range. Can be 0.
TAGSTAGS are special abilties, rules, and other effects.

Turn Phases

1. Initiative

Players determine which force must activate first in the Movement Phase and Combat Phases. Damage is simultaneous in LANDWAR and destroyed units are all removed together. Initiative plays an important role in positioning your forces, reacting to other players' movements, and being able to prioritize attacks in later phases.

2. Movement

In this phase, all players take turns moving any, all, or none of their Units. Each player must complete all their units' move actions before the next player activates their units. Order of player activation is based on who won the Initiative phase.

3. Combat

During this phase, units with the ability to make attacks may attempt to do so. Successful attacks will deal damage to target units. As in the Movement Phase each player makes and resolves their units attacks before the next player may do so. Unlike the Movement Phase, the player that has Advantage always resolves their attacks first.

4. Damage

For this phase, players apply any and all damage that their units took during the Combat Phase. Damage is applied simultaneously for all players units. Casualties and destroyed units are then removed during this step. Effects and Tags that might affect damage or taking damage are also used now.

5. Resolution

The final phase of a turn, Units make their Stress check if they have any amount of Stress tokens and are not Panicked currently. Players also check the Scenario game rules to see if victory for a player has been achieved. Any scenario with objectives, the scoring for objectives takes place at this time. If victory has not been achieved, players go back to Initiative Phase and begin the next turn of LANDWAR!

Initiative Phase

Players determine who will activate their units during the turn's phases.

  1. Panicked: Units with this token:
      remove 1 Stress Point.
  2. Each side rolls 3D6 and totals their results.
    • Each player rolls their own Initiative regardless of any alliances.
    • Side with higher total has the Advantage.

      See Reference for multiplayer initiative.

    • IF the totals are tied, player that lost last turn gets Advantage.
  3. -1 INI for each Unit that is Panicked
  4. Resolve any Initiative effects or events now.
  5. Player with Advantage chooses Moving first or last.

Movement Phase

  1. Each Player moves any or all of their available Units at once, based on initiative order of players.
  2. Each Unit must complete their move before the player can move another Unit.
  3. When moving a Unit, the controlling Player can select one of the following options:
    • Declare Stationary.
      • Unit may Rotate to a new Facing.
      • Unit gains +1 ATK for this Combat Phase.
      • Unit cannot use its Evade bonus for this Combat Phase.
    • Make Normal move.
      • Unit may move in any direction up to to their current Move value.
      • Unit cannot move through an enemy Unit.
      • Unit may change their Facing at the end of the move.
      • Unit may use its Evade value IF it moves more than 1/4 of its total Move value.
    • Use TAG rules, if Players are using TAG special rules for units, and a Unit has a TAG that allows specific movement, it can be used here.
  4. Complete selected Movement and check for these rules:

Unless otherwise specified, Units move 1/2 their Move value when:

  • Moving through intervening terrain.
  • Starting move inside terrain.

Whenever a Unit completes a Normal move where the total distance moved was 14" or more, the Unit has completed a High Speed Move.

Units during their this Combat Phase are given the following effects.

  • -2ATK (Minimum of 1).
  • re-roll 2 DEFdice.

When a Unit starts their Move 2" or less from at least 1 Enemy Unit that has Melee greater than 0

  • Unit may only move half their current Move value rounded up.
  • Unit suffers +1 Stress.
  • Unit suffers -1 ATK for Ranged Attacks this turn.
  • Targeting any Unit that was in Melee Range before Unit's move, Unit suffers additional -1 ATK for Ranged Attacks.

Unit potentially suffers +1 Stress when trying to flank an enemy unit.

  • Panicked Units cannot attempt Flanking.
  • When a Unit moves from the Forward arc to the Rear arc of an Enemy Unit.
    • IF the Unit was in Target's Line of Sight at the start and end of the move.
    • IF the Unit ends their Move 8" or less from the Target Unit.

Combat Phase

Player with Advantage resolves all their attacks first. Attacks are split into 2 categories - Range and Melee. Damage from all Attacks are not applied until the Resolution Phase!

Ranged Attacks

  • Attacking Unit is not currently in Melee Range(1") of any Enemy Unit.
  • Attacking Unit must have Line of Sight to the Target Unit.
  • Target must be the closest Unit to the Attacker in Line of Sight.
    • See Focus Fire below this section.
  • Target cannot be 2" or less from any friendly model.
  • TAG rules may override, add, or change these qualifications!

Melee Attacks

  • Unit must have a Melee Damage stat greater than 0.
  • Target must be in Melee Range of Unit. default 1".
  • Target must be in the Attacker's Forward Arc.

There are only 3 main Range Brackets in the game.

  • Melee is 0" to 1".
  • Effective Range, default is 16".
    • Units can have their own Effective Range using the Range stat on their Info Card.
  • Long Range, unlimited distance.
    • Target gains +1 DEF dice.

When a Unit makes a Ranged Attack they can select the non-closest valid Target under the following limits:

  • Unit cannot be Panicked.
  • Unit Stress level must be below the Unit's max. - default is 6
  • IF closest enemy Unit is in Effective Range, and desired Target is at Long Range:
    • Unit suffers +2 Stress for each attack that misses.
  • IF closest enemy Unit is under 6" and desired Target is > 6" range:
    • Unit suffers +1 Stress for each attack that misses.
  • Starting Amount: 3D6.
  • Apply any TAG rules to the ATK amount.

When the Unit has a Panic token on it during the Combat Phase

  • total ATK Dice are halved, minimum of 1.

When the Target is 2" to 4" away.

  • Attacker gains +1 ATK.

When the attacker was Stationary during the Movement Phase

  • Attacker gains +1 ATK.
  • Starting Amount: 3D6
  • Apply any TAG rules to the DEF amount.

When the Target has a Panic token on it during the Combat Phase

  • total DEF Dice are halved, minimum of 1.

When the Target is at Long Range

  • Target may +1 DEF dice to this roll.

If Target is behind or in a board object agreed as 'Cover':

  • Defender gains +1 Evasion for each Attack against the target. These cannot be used later.
  • OR Defender may re-roll half their total dice, but Cover object is removed from board!

If Target was not Stationary during this Turn's Movement Phase

If Target moved 1/4(25%) or more of their current MOVE.

  • Target may add up to unit's EVADE value to their DEF roll results.
  • Evade points are spent per Turn, once they're all spent, they only recharge if Unit moves next Turn!

If Attacker moved 14" or more during this Turn's Movement Phase:

  • Defender may re-roll up to 2 DEF dice.
  • Starting Amount: 3D6
  • Stationary: token gives Attacker +2 ATK.
  • Apply any TAG rules to the ATK amount.

When the Unit has a Panic token on it during the Combat Phase

  • total ATK Dice are halved, minimum of 1.

If Attacker started outside Melee Range and moved 6" or more this Turn into Melee Range:

  • For this Combat Phase +1 ATK
  • Starting Amount: 3D6
  • Check High Speed Move, Cover, Evasion.
  • Apply any TAG rules to the DEF amount.

When the Unit has a Panic token on it during the Combat Phase

  • total DEF Dice are halved, minimum of 1.

If Attacker moved 14" or more during this Turn's Movement Phase:

  • Defender may re-roll up to 2 DEF dice.

If Target was not Stationary during this Turn's Movement Phase

If Target moved 1/4(25%) or more of their current MOVE.

  • Target may add up to unit's EVADE value to their DEF roll results.
  • Evade points are spent per Turn, once they're all spent, they only recharge if Unit moves next Turn!

Resolve Attacks

Now Players assemble the dice they will need to roll to see if the Attack is successful or not.

Damage Phase

  • Attacker and Defender roll their D6s.
  • Attacker: each 4+ is 1 'success'.
  • Defender: each 5+ is 1 'success'.
  • Compare the two rolls:
  • For Each Defender Success, remove 1 Attacker Success.
  • If Attacker has 1 or more Success remaining.
    • Defender suffers an amount of DMG equal to the Attacker's DMG value for that Range.
  • Check Target Destroyed.
  • Check Crew Shaken
  • Check Suppressing Fire.

When the Defenders Armor stat is reduced to 0:

  • The unit is now considered Destroyed.
  • Do Not remove the unit until the Resolution Phase of this Turn.
  • The Destroyed unit may still make their final attacks during this Combat Phase.
  • Each remaining friendly Unit in LoS and 6" suffers +1 Stress point.

When a Unit has taken any Damage this turn:

  • +1 Stress if Armor less than or equal to 50% starting total.
  • +1 Stress if Armor less than or equal to 25% starting total.
  • effects are cumulative.

If Defender was attacked (regardless of hits) by 3 or more enemy Units this turn:

  • Defender suffers +2 Stress Point.

Players continue to make and resolve attacks as needed or declared until any-or-all Units that can attack, have done so.

Resolution Phase

All damage from the Combat Phase is applied now.

Units that have been destroyed are removed at this time.

  • Check Panic and Stress.
  • Check for Victory Conditions.

Any non-Destroyed Unit with 1 or more Stress Tokens must make a Stress Check.

  • note: TAGS may be applied for Stress Checks and tokens at this time.
  • Controlling Player rolls 1D6 for each of their Units with Stress Tokens.
  • If the roll is equal or higher than the current Stress Tokens for that Unit:
    • Player removes all Stress Tokens from the Unit.
  • If the roll is less than the number of Stress Tokens for the Unit:
    • Unit has become Panicked.
    • Place a Panicked token next to the Unit or on its data card.

At the end of the entire turn, players will check if the Victory Conditions for the match have been satisfied.

  • If conditions are met, the game is over!
  • If conditions are not met, proceed back to the Initiative Phase to begin a new Turn.

REFERENCE

Finally, at the end for reference are some important concepts that are used during gameplay.

TAGS are an optional set of special rules for enhancing Units in your army.

  • A TAG will declare when it can be used or applied.
  • Each Unit can only use each of its TAGS once per Turn.
  • Adding and Subtracting dice to a roll must happen before any rolls are made.
  • Each dice in a situation can only be re-rolled ONCE.
  • Keyword: must re-roll; Player must re-roll all dice up to the number required.
  • Keyword: may Player can choose a number of dice to re-roll up to the set total.
  • Dice Modifiers are applied AFTER re-rolls.

Players or Scenarios may have situations where multiple players are allied with each other.

  • As stated, each player rolls their Initiative roll separately including their army's modifiers.
  • Initiative order is then based on the comparison of every player's roll, with lowest player going first.
  • The player with Advantage cannot grant this status on any of their allies (unless otherwise specified).
  • The player with Advantage can elect to move first but to do this, all players reverse the initiative order.

Primary method to determine if a Unit can see other Units, usually for Attacks.

  • Draw a line from the center of the model of the Unit.
  • to the center of the model of any target Units.
  • If the line is not obstructed by enemy models or Terrain Objects, then Line of Sight is achieved.

Terrain for LANDWAR can be varied and complex, its completely up to the players and materials they have available.

Basic Rules

  • For each Terrain Object Players must agree on its general properties:
    • Does the object block Line of Sight?
    • Can Units move into and out of the object?
